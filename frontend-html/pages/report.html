Analysis Report

Executive Summary

This report provides a comprehensive analysis of public opinion dynamics observed during the 120-round simulation. The system monitored 64 autonomous agents across five major social platforms, tracking sentiment evolution, message propagation patterns, and emerging narrative clusters.

Overall sentiment trajectory shifted from neutral-positive to moderately negative following the simulated policy announcement at Round 38. Peak engagement occurred between Rounds 42 and 56, with Twitter and Weibo serving as primary amplification channels.

Key Findings

  • Sentiment polarity inverted within 8 rounds after the triggering event, faster than historical baselines.
  • A small cohort of 6 high-influence agents drove 62% of total reach, indicating concentrated opinion leadership.
  • Cross-platform migration was observed: discussions originating on Reddit migrated to Twitter within 3-4 rounds.
  • Counter-narratives emerged spontaneously at Round 51, partially mitigating negative sentiment by Round 68.
  • Engagement velocity correlated strongly with message emotional intensity (r = 0.74, p < 0.001).

Sentiment Analysis

Platform-level sentiment decomposition reveals heterogeneous reactions. Twitter exhibited the most volatile sentiment curve, oscillating between 0.35 and -0.42 on the normalized polarity scale. Weibo demonstrated stronger negative drift, bottoming at -0.51 before gradual recovery.

Facebook and LinkedIn showed comparatively muted responses, suggesting professional-network contexts may dampen emotional contagion. Reddit displayed bimodal sentiment distribution, reflecting polarized sub-community reactions rather than consensus movement.

Temporal clustering analysis identified three distinct sentiment regimes: pre-announcement stability (Rounds 1-37), post-announcement turbulence (Rounds 38-78), and adaptive equilibration (Rounds 79-120).

Recommendation

Based on simulation outcomes, early intervention within the first 12 rounds post-event is critical for narrative steering. Delayed response beyond Round 50 faces substantially diminished returns due to entrenched opinion crystallization.

Targeting the identified high-influence agent cohort with tailored messaging yields the highest leverage for sentiment redirection. Platform-specific strategies are recommended: rapid-response teams for Twitter/Weibo, community moderation for Reddit, and authority amplification for LinkedIn.

Subsequent simulation iterations should test intervention timing at Rounds 40, 45, and 50 to establish optimal response windows and dose-response relationships for planned counter-narrative deployment.
